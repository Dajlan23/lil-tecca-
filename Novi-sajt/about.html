About Lil Tecca

Lil Tecca is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter from Queens, New York. He first gained popularity with his hit single "Ran$om" in 2019. His debut studio album "We Love You Tecca" was released on August 30, 2019 and reached number 4 on the US Billboard 200 chart. Lil Tecca has since released several successful singles and collaborations, and continues to tour and perform around the world.

Born Tyler-Justin Anthony Sharpe on August 26, 2002, Lil Tecca grew up in Springfield Gardens, a neighborhood in Queens, New York. He began making music in his early teens, initially releasing songs on the music-sharing platform SoundCloud.

"Ran$om" was Lil Tecca's breakthrough hit, peaking at number 4 on the US Billboard Hot 100 chart and going on to achieve multi-platinum status. The song was included on his debut mixtape, "We Love You Tecca," which was released on August 30, 2019. The mixtape also featured the popular tracks "Did It Again" and "Love Me."

Since the release of "We Love You Tecca," Lil Tecca has continued to release successful singles and collaborations, including "Out of Love" and "Dolly" featuring Lil Uzi Vert. He has also collaborated with a variety of other artists, including Juice WRLD, Internet Money, and Lil Mosey. In addition to his music career, Lil Tecca has also expressed an interest in fashion, and has been seen wearing clothing from brands such as Supreme and Off-White.

Lil Tecca has been praised for his unique style and sound, which incorporates elements of trap, hip hop, and R&B. He has cited a variety of artists as influences, including Kanye West, Travis Scott, and Lil Wayne. Despite his young age, Lil Tecca has already achieved significant success in the music industry, and continues to be one of the most exciting and promising artists in hip hop today.
